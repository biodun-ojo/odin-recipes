description

Fufu is a staple food in many countries and is often eaten with soups, stews, and sauces. It's a very filling dish and is a great source of carbohydrates.

ingredent

  • Cassava: Also known as yuca, this root vegetable is a staple starch in many parts of the world.
  • Plantains: These are firm, starchy vegetables that are related to bananas. Green plantains are typically used for fufu.
  • Yams: Another root vegetable that can be used to make fufu.

steps

  • Prepare the starchy base: This involves peeling, washing, and cooking the cassava, plantains, yams, or other chosen starch. Boiling or steaming are common cooking methods.
  • Mash or pound the cooked starch: Traditionally, a mortar and pestle are used to achieve a smooth and elastic dough.
  • Optional: Add water or other ingredients (depending on the recipe) to adjust the consistency of the dough.
  • Serve: Fufu is typically molded into balls and eaten with soups, stews, or sauces.
