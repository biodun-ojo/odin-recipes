description

Amala is a popular Nigerian dish made from yam flour. It's a doughy side dish similar to fufu. Amala is often eaten with soups, stews, and sauces.

ingredients

  • flour: This is the main ingredient and gives amala its characteristic texture. Yam flour is made from peeled, dried, and ground yams.
  • Water: Hot water is used to cook the yam flour and form the dough.

steps

  • water: You'll need a pot of boiling water.
  • Add yam flour: Gradually whisk the yam flour into the boiling water while stirring constantly. This helps prevent lumps from forming.
  • Stir and cook: Keep stirring the mixture until it thickens and becomes doughy. You may need to adjust the heat to medium or low to prevent burning.
  • Serve: Enjoy your amala with your favorite soup or stew!
